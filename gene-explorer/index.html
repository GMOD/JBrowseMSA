JBrowse integration

Type any human gene to build a connected JBrowse session: a collapsed-intron genome view, its 100-way protein alignment, and the AlphaFold structure.

How this works

The page resolves the gene to its canonical (MANE / RefSeq Select) transcript and parses the GFF in your browser. There is nocollapseIntrons option in JBrowse — the trick is to give the Linear Genome View a loc made of the exon ranges, space-separated, so each renders back-to-back and the introns disappear. Everything is computed live from public data (mygene.info, the UCSC RefSeq Select GFF over tabix, AlphaFold); only the alignment slice comes from a hosted file.
